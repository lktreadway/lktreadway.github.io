Hobbies

A well-rounded person enjoys many past-times.
My interests are wide and varied, but here is a sample of things I enjoy doing in my spare time.

Music

My favorite genres include Jazz, Classic Rock, and R&B.

Music has always been part of my life. I have experimented with various instruments, but percussion is my soul instrument. I love rhythm's paradoxical qualities of complexity and simplicity. Rhythm is foundational, strong, and supportive. Without rhythm, there is no music, no life. As with any foundation, if it crumbles, so does everything else.

I have been singing since I was very small. Throughout, primary school, college, and adulthood, I have found ways to lend my voice to various choirs. For instance, my church produced an album where I provided backup vocals. It is an awesome worship CD called Season of Praise. Check it out here to listen to some sound clips.

Reading

I am a prolific reader. I always have a book in hand. It's not uncommon for me to be reading
multiple books at once. I enjoy both fiction (romance, comedy, Sci-fi, etc.) and non-fiction (history, self-help, psychology, etc.).

I belong to two online book clubs at present. My monthly club is currently reading The Diary of a Bookseller by Shaun Bythell. My other club is weekly and currently working through Atomic Habits by James Clear.

Photography

I have enjoyed photography since I was a kid. My first photo was of a sailboat on a lake at camp one summer when I was about eight years old.

I took photography in eighth grade and became the photographer for the school paper (grades 8-10). This included developing photos in the darkroom, doing layout, and writing stories.

After college, I took continuing ed courses at Indiana University Purdue University at Indianapolis (IUPUI) and gained a certificate in photography, which also included work in the darkroom.

Later, I built a darkroom in my bathroom. A friend of mine, from a local photographer's group, and I, developed a photo that won "Best of Show" at the Indiana State Fair. My own color photo was hung in that show. Just to have your work hung was an honor as not many made it that far.

The carousel below shows all flowers, but I like other nature shots too.
Mostly natural formations such as the sky, clouds, and landscapes.
I also love architecture. The clean lines and how they interact with a structure's surroundings can be a beautiful thing.
